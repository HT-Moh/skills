# Rewrite Prompts

`phrases.md` and `structures.md` tell you *what* to remove. This file tells you *how* to
rewrite it. Each entry names a pattern to spot and gives a specific rewrite instruction
you can apply to a draft (or hand to an LLM).

Usage: scan the draft for the pattern, then apply the matching instruction. Keep the
author's meaning; change only the slop.

## Throat-Clearing Openers

**Spot:** "Here's the thing", "Let's dive in", "In today's fast-paced world", "It's worth noting that", "Buckle up".
**Rewrite:** Delete the opener. Start with the first real claim. If the sentence still works after cutting the first clause, the clause was throat-clearing.

## Overused AI Words

**Spot:** delve, tapestry, crucial, leverage, comprehensive, robust, seamless, realm, landscape, testament, beacon, navigate, foster, intricate.
**Rewrite:** Replace with the plain word. `leverage → use`, `delve into → examine`, `comprehensive → complete` (or cut), `robust → strong` (or name the actual property), `tapestry/realm/landscape → the specific thing`. If no plain word fits, the sentence is decoration. Cut it.

## "Not X but Y" Contrasts

**Spot:** "It's not about speed, it's about consistency", "This isn't a bug, it's a feature", "not just X, but Y".
**Rewrite:** State Y directly and drop the X half. "It's not about speed, it's about consistency" becomes "Consistency is what matters here." The negated setup adds nothing.

## Rule of Three

**Spot:** Three parallel items used for rhythm: "fast, cheap, and reliable", "we build, we ship, we learn".
**Rewrite:** Cut to two, or to the one that carries weight. Two items read as deliberate; three reads as a template.

## Nominalizations

**Spot:** A verb hidden inside a noun: "make a decision", "provide an explanation", "perform a calculation", "do an analysis of".
**Rewrite:** Turn the noun back into a verb. "make a decision → decide", "provide an explanation → explain", "perform a calculation → calculate". Shorter and active.

## Filler Transitions

**Spot:** furthermore, moreover, however, additionally, in conclusion, that being said, it is important to note.
**Rewrite:** Delete the transition and let the sentences sit next to each other. If the logic breaks without it, the problem is the argument order, not the missing word. Reorder instead.

## Vague Authority

**Spot:** "studies show", "experts agree", "it is widely recognized", "research suggests", "many believe".
**Rewrite:** Name the source and the finding, or cut the claim. "Studies show X" becomes "A 2025 Stanford study found X" or it goes. An unnamed authority is no authority.

## Passive Voice

**Spot:** "mistakes were made", "the file is processed by the script", "it was decided".
**Rewrite:** Find the actor and make it the subject. "mistakes were made → we made mistakes", "the file is processed by the script → the script processes the file".

## False Agency

**Spot:** An inanimate thing doing a human action: "the data tells a story", "the complaint becomes a fix", "the decision emerges".
**Rewrite:** Put a person in the subject seat. "the data tells a story → we found X in the data", "the decision emerges → the team decided".

## Hedging Stacks

**Spot:** Piled softeners: "it might perhaps be somewhat possible that", "this could potentially help in some cases".
**Rewrite:** State the claim once, at the confidence you actually hold. "This could potentially help in some cases" becomes "This helps when X."

## Stilted / Overly Formal Language

**Spot:** Archaic or ceremonial words (henceforth, heretofore, whilst, verily, hereby) and pompous Latinate verbs (utilize, commence, facilitate, ascertain).
**Rewrite:** Say it the way a person would. "utilize → use", "commence → start", "ascertain → find out", "in accordance with → under". See `phrases.md`.

## Quotable Bait

**Spot:** A line engineered to sound like a pull-quote: "And that changes everything", "The future is already here".
**Rewrite:** Replace the slogan with the concrete claim it was dressing up. If there is no concrete claim underneath, cut the line.
